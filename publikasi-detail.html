Reaffirming Multilateralism: A Proposal for ASEAN Common Framework for Negotiating with the US

Dewan Ekonomi Nasional
15 min read
PDF Available

Executive Summary

"Necessary to synchronize between ASEAN countries becomes all ID ASEAN countries are affected by the US reciprocal tariff policy as in the statement from Indonesian Coordinating Board Minister, Airlangga Hartarto during his visit to Malaysia first day after the announcement of 'Liberation Day' Trump by The US."

Introduction

President Donald Trump, ASEAN countries are among those countries dealing with the threat of high "reciprocal tariff" set from the US. These bilateral actions implement broad and comprehensive policies that will affect not only trade but also the region's economic and security concerns in general.

Current Challenges

Recognizing the gravity of the situation, just a week after the announcement ASEAN Economic Ministers issued a joint ministerial statement affirming the commitment to regional, operational trade, dispute resolution through multilateral frameworks, and coordination in response to external economic shocks. While the statement shows aspirations to collectively responding to the trade and geoeconomic dynamics, it fell short on its realization.

Key Policy Recommendations

  • Establish unified ASEAN negotiation framework
  • Coordinate tariff response strategies
  • Strengthen multilateral trade mechanisms
  • Enhance regional economic integration

ASEAN Response Strategy

This policy note encourages ASEAN member states to move from collective response operationalized by identifying and coordinating key policy areas then engaging in negotiations with the United States. In particular, this note highlights the importance of aligning critical elements of the negotiations such as tariffs, non-tariff barriers, rules of origin, and other trade-related regulatory frameworks, especially to maintain tracking principles under the WTO. Greater alignment on these issues will help ASEAN present a cohesive, credible, and strong position in dealing with the US.

Implementation Framework

Recently, Trump announced a plan to implement reciprocal tariffs starting August 1 for several partners, including ASEAN. This ASEAN should not have engaged negotiations with the US individually; it highlights the importance of adopting a harmonized approach between the US and other partners, as well as the need to strengthen ASEAN's integration.
